Let's embark on this artistic journey together." Art is a diverse range of human activity and its resulting product that involves creative or imaginative talent generally expressive of technical proficiency, beauty, emotional power, or conceptual ideas. There is no generally agreed definition of what constitutes art, and its interpretation has varied greatly throughout history and across cultures. In the Western tradition, the three classical branches of visual art are painting, sculpture, and architecture. Our art gallery

Our Vision

Driven by a passion for art in all its forms,Alchemy strives to foster an environment where artists can thrive and art lovers can discover new inspirations. Our vision is to curate thought-provoking exhibitions that challenge perspectives and ignite conversations, connecting artists with audiences in meaningful ways. Art galleries contribute significantly to the cultural landscape of a community. They can show art from their area or from around the world. This gives the public a chance to see and appreciate works of art that they might not have been able to see or appreciate otherwise. Art galleries are not just buildings with artworks on display; they are vital to the art world and society. They are an important part of the cultural landscape because they give artists a place to show off their work and bring together people who love art. These spaces have been critical in shaping the evolution of the art world by promoting artistic expression and advancing creativity.

Discover Our Collection

Explore a curated selection of paintings, sculptures, and mixed-media artworks that embody innovation, diversity, and craftsmanship. From emerging talents to established names, each piece in our collection tells a unique story, inviting you to delve deeper into the creative process and the essence of human experience. Our stunning Grade 1 listed Picture Gallery houses the Thomas Holloway collection of Victorian paintings, and we hold temporary exhibitions in a dedicated space in the Davison Building. The University's art collection includes world-class paintings, sculptures, prints, drawings and watercolours including works by William Powell Frith, John Everett Millais and Edward Burne-Jones. Recent additions to the collection include works by Augustus John and Graham Sutherland.
